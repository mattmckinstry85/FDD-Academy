The Qualification Framework

Four quadrants. Use this to structure every discovery call. Click any question to see why it matters and what good looks like.

1
Needs
How many FDD or QoE engagements does your team run per year?
How many people are in your transaction services team?
What is the typical fee range for an FDD engagement at your firm?
What deal size do you typically work on — EV range?
What are your growth targets for the practice this year?
Are you looking to grow headcount or grow revenue per head?
2
Pain
How long does it take from data room access to first cut of the EBITDA bridge?
What percentage of engagement hours are spent on data processing versus analysis?
How confident are you in the consistency of outputs across different team members?
If nothing changes in your process over the next 12 months, what does that mean for the team?
Have you ever had to turn down work or delay a report because of capacity?
3
Sources
Walk me through how your team currently processes a new data room — what happens in the first 48 hours?
What tools does your team use today — beyond Excel?
Do you outsource any part of the analytical work — offshore, nearshore, or to juniors?
How much of the initial data processing falls on your junior staff?
Do you have standardised templates, or does each manager build their own approach?
4
Solution
What if you could get a structured first cut of the EBITDA bridge within hours of uploading the data room?
If your team got back 8-12 hours per engagement, what would you do with that capacity?
How important is it that every engagement follows the same analytical structure regardless of who runs it?
Would it make sense to run PinpointAI alongside your next engagement so you can see the output before committing?
Who else in the firm would need to be involved in a decision like this?
Why ask this
What the answer tells you
Order matters
Always start with Needs before Pain. Understand their world first. If you go to pain too early you sound like every other vendor.
Let them talk
The best qualification calls are 70% them, 30% you. Ask a question, then be quiet. The detail comes in the silence.
Only pitch after pain
Do not move to the Solution quadrant until you have confirmed real pain in quadrant 2. A solution without a problem is just noise.
